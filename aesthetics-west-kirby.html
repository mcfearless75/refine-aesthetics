What Makes a Qualified Aesthetics Practitioner?

The aesthetics industry in the UK is not uniformly regulated. Some practitioners hold full clinical qualifications; others operate after weekend courses. Knowing what to look for protects your safety and your investment.

At minimum, look for a practitioner who can demonstrate:

  • A relevant medical, nursing, pharmacy or science-level qualification
  • Completion of accredited aesthetics training at Level 7 or equivalent
  • Membership of a recognised professional body such as the JCCP, Save Face or equivalent
  • A transparent pre-treatment consultation process with a medical history review
  • Clear aftercare protocols and an emergency protocol for adverse events
  • Insurance through a specialist aesthetics insurer
