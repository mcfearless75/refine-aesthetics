Is Profhilo Worth It?
A Wirral Client's Guide

By Samantha Casey, Advanced Aesthetics Practitioner  ·  Refine Aesthetics, West Kirby  ·  June 2026

Profhilo is one of the most asked-about treatments at Refine Aesthetics. Clients from across Wirral — West Kirby, Hoylake, Heswall, Neston and beyond — come in having heard about it from friends or seen it mentioned online, but with understandable questions: what exactly is it doing, is it actually worth the cost, and is it right for my skin?

This is a straightforward guide to Profhilo — what it is, how it works, what results look like and who it suits. No filler (no pun intended).

What is Profhilo?

Profhilo is a bio-remodelling injectable made from ultra-pure, high-concentration hyaluronic acid. It is not a dermal filler — it does not add volume or change the shape of your face. Instead, it works by releasing HA slowly into the surrounding tissue, deeply hydrating the skin and stimulating four types of collagen and elastin.

The result is skin that looks and feels fundamentally better — more hydrated, firmer, smoother and more luminous. Think of it as the difference between painting a wall and improving the plasterwork underneath.

Profhilo is particularly effective for people in their mid-30s and beyond who notice their skin lacks the density and glow it once had — even with a good skincare routine. It addresses the underlying biology, not just the surface.

How does the treatment work?

Profhilo is injected using a technique called BAP (Bio Aesthetic Points) — five specific anatomical points on each side of the face. This placement allows the product to flow through the tissue and distribute evenly, rather than sitting in one spot as a filler would.

The treatment takes under 20 minutes. There are no cannulas, no fanning, no deep tissue manipulation. Most clients notice minimal bruising compared to filler treatments, though some swelling at the injection points for 24–48 hours is normal.

The standard protocol is two sessions, four weeks apart. After that, a single maintenance session every six months is typically sufficient to sustain the results.

Who is Profhilo right for?

  • Anyone noticing skin laxity or crepiness, particularly around the lower face, neck and décolletage
  • Clients who want skin quality improvements without volume changes
  • Those with dry, dull or thin skin who want a deeper fix than topical skincare can provide
  • Clients in their mid-30s to 60s looking for a preventative or corrective approach to ageing skin
  • Anyone who has plateaued with their skincare routine and wants a clinical step up

Profhilo is also suitable for the neck, décolletage and hands — areas where filler would be inappropriate but where skin quality decline is often noticeable first.

Profhilo vs dermal filler — what's the difference?

This is the question I get most often. The short answer: they do completely different things and are not in competition. Filler adds structure and volume — it is ideal for the lips, cheeks, jawline and chin. Profhilo improves the quality of the skin itself — hydration, tone, firmness and luminosity.

Many clients at Refine Aesthetics have both. The combination works well: filler restores or defines the architecture of the face, and Profhilo ensures the skin that sits over that structure looks genuinely healthy.
