What is EXCEED Precision Microneedling?

Microneedling is a controlled skin resurfacing treatment that uses fine, precisely calibrated needles to create micro-channels in the dermis. These channels trigger the skin's natural wound-healing response, stimulating collagen and elastin production. The result, over a course of sessions, is measurably improved skin texture, reduced scarring, softer fine lines, and a more even tone.

At Refine Aesthetics & Skin Clinic in West Kirby, Samantha Casey uses the EXCEED Medical Microneedling device — a CE-marked, FDA-cleared system with dual-speed motor technology for consistent needle penetration and minimal skin trauma. This is medical-grade equipment, not a home roller or a consumer beauty device. That distinction matters for both safety and results.

EXCEED is particularly well regarded for its precision at depth. Needle penetration can be adjusted from 0.5mm to 3mm depending on the target concern, allowing Samantha to treat everything from fine surface lines to deeper acne ice-pick scarring in a single protocol.

Skin Concerns Microneedling Treats

Clients travelling from across the Wirral to Refine Aesthetics are often dealing with concerns they have struggled to address with topical skincare alone. Microneedling reaches the dermis directly, which is why it outperforms most creams for structural concerns.

  • Acne scarring, including rolling, boxcar, and shallow ice-pick scars
  • Fine lines and early dynamic wrinkles around the eyes, mouth, and forehead
  • Large pores and uneven skin texture
  • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation and melasma (when combined with appropriate homecare)
  • Skin laxity and loss of elasticity, particularly in the mid-face and neck
  • Stretch marks on the body, including the abdomen and thighs
  • Overall dullness and lack of radiance in sun-damaged skin

A Note on Realistic Outcomes

Microneedling is a genuine collagen-induction treatment but it is not a single-session fix. Structural improvements — particularly for scarring and laxity — require a minimum course of three sessions spaced four weeks apart. Clients who book one session and expect dramatic results tend to be disappointed. Clients who commit to a course see real, lasting change. Samantha will discuss the right number of sessions for your specific concern at consultation.

What to Expect at Your Appointment

Your first visit to the clinic at 56 Grange Road, West Kirby begins with a full skin assessment. Samantha will review your medical history, assess your skin type and the target concerns, and confirm the needle depth and protocol suited to your skin. There is no obligation to proceed on the day.

On treatment day, a topical anaesthetic cream is applied and left for 30 to 40 minutes. The EXCEED device is then passed systematically across the treatment area using consistent, overlapping strokes. A standard full-face treatment takes around 45 minutes in the chair, not including numbing time.

Immediately after treatment the skin will look flushed and feel warm, similar to mild sunburn. This typically settles within 24 to 48 hours. Light redness can persist for up to 72 hours in clients with more reactive skin. Downtime is minimal compared to laser alternatives, which is one reason microneedling has become the preferred resurfacing choice for clients from Hoylake, Heswall, and Neston who need to return to work quickly.

Aftercare is straightforward: no active ingredients (retinol, acids, vitamin C) for five days, physical SPF every morning, and minimal makeup for 24 hours. Samantha provides a written aftercare guide at every appointment.

Combining Microneedling With Other Treatments

For clients whose main concerns are both texture and laxity, microneedling pairs well with Profhilo (from £220). Profhilo works in the deeper dermis as a bio-remodelling agent, improving skin quality and firmness over two sessions. Microneedling then refines the surface. The two are staggered two weeks apart to avoid overlapping healing responses.
